Ajanta Caves

The Ajanta Caves, also known as the Caves of the Crescent Moon, are a series of 29 ancient Buddhist rock-cut cave monuments. These caves are located in the western Satpuras of Maharashtra, India. They are dated to have been built between the 2nd century BCE and the 5th century CE.

Unforgettable Sensations

Visiting the Ajanta Caves provides an opportunity to experience and appreciate the intricate beauty of ancient Indian art and architecture. It is highly recommended to visit these exceptional caves to enhance your understanding of the rich cultural heritage of India.

In addition to the aforementioned caves, the Ajanta Caves complex also features the Flight of the Eagles, a breathtaking panel painting depicting a procession of animals.
